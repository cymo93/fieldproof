# FieldProof Strategy

## Core Thesis

FieldProof should start as the tool brands use to show where food came from and what was checked on the farm.

The first product should not try to replace certification. It should help brands, producers, and verifiers turn field visits into customer-facing product stories, reusable photos and clips, and clear review notes.

The wedge:

> Farm visits that produce useful proof and beautiful content at the same time.

## Why This Wedge

Most traceability tools answer: "Where did this product come from?"

Most certification systems answer: "Did this producer meet a standard?"

FieldProof should answer a more commercially useful early question:

> "Can this brand show customers recent farm photos, plain-language checks, and a story worth scanning?"

That creates a practical flywheel:

1. Brands want trust, differentiation, and better origin content.
2. QR product stories bring customers into the story at purchase or consumption.
3. Scan and engagement data show brands which origins consumers care about.
4. Brands fund more field visits.
5. Producers gain reputation and a stronger market signal.
6. Verifiers get paid for skilled fieldwork.
7. More farms and product batches become publicly legible.

## First Market

Recommended first market: specialty coffee.

Why:

- Origin already matters to consumers.
- Roasters already tell producer and cooperative stories.
- Packaging can carry QR codes.
- Coffee has both premium storytelling demand and traceability pressure.
- Verification can start lightweight, then grow toward compliance and certification support.

Secondary markets:

- Cacao and chocolate.
- Olive oil.
- Honey.
- Tea.
- Spices.
- Wine.

## First Product

FieldProof Studio.

Surfaces:

- Product story pages.
- Field visit app.
- Checklist library.
- Producer profiles.
- Media asset library.

The customer-facing story page creates demand. The field visit app creates supply. The checklist library keeps the work honest.

## Positioning

Do say:

- Show customers where this food came from.
- Turn farm visits into product stories and marketing assets.
- Collect field photos, notes, and samples against clear checklists.
- Create review packets that can support certification later.

Do not lead with:

- Blockchain certification replacement.
- Full regenerative certification.
- Consumer app.
- Compliance software.

## What To Build First

Build the smallest profitable slice:

1. A brand creates a product batch story page.
2. A verifier completes a guided field visit.
3. The visit follows one or more standards-based checklists.
4. The brand approves public media and story copy.
5. The product QR code points customers to the story page.
6. The brand can download approved assets for marketing.

## Pricing Hypothesis

Keep pricing below traditional certification and content-production budgets.

Possible pricing:

- $49-$199/month for small brand story-page hosting.
- $150-$750 per origin capture visit, depending on geography and depth.
- 10-20% marketplace take rate on verifier work.
- $500-$2,000/month for co-ops, importers, or brands managing many products.

The first budget line is not "audit software." It is a blend of origin marketing, traceability, and lightweight review.

## Strategic Constraint

FieldProof must avoid trust theater.

The product story should clearly distinguish:

- Producer story.
- Self-reported claim.
- Checked in the field.
- Lab results attached.
- Reviewed by a third party.
- Disputed or needs-revisit claim.

This keeps the product useful before formal certification while protecting trust.
