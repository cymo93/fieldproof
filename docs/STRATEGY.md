# FieldProof Strategy

## Core Thesis

FieldProof should start as the media-and-proof layer for regenerative and high-integrity food sourcing.

The first product should not try to replace certification. It should help brands, producers, and verifiers turn credible field visits into consumer-facing proof pages and reusable marketing assets.

The wedge:

> Science-rooted capture workflows that produce marketing-grade proof assets.

## Why This Wedge

Most traceability tools answer: "Where did this product come from?"

Most certification systems answer: "Did this producer meet a standard?"

FieldProof should answer a more commercially useful early question:

> "Can this brand show consumers beautiful, credible, recent evidence of the farm practices behind this product?"

That creates a practical flywheel:

1. Brands want trust, differentiation, and better origin content.
2. QR proof pages bring consumers into the story at purchase or consumption.
3. Scan and engagement data show brands which origins consumers care about.
4. Brands fund more field capture and verification.
5. Producers gain reputation and a stronger market signal.
6. Verifiers get paid for skilled, science-rooted fieldwork.
7. More farms and product batches become publicly legible.

## First Market

Recommended first market: specialty coffee.

Why:

- Origin already matters to consumers.
- Roasters already tell producer and cooperative stories.
- Packaging can carry QR codes.
- Coffee has both premium storytelling demand and traceability pressure.
- Verification can start lightweight, then grow toward compliance and certification support.

Secondary markets:

- Cacao and chocolate.
- Olive oil.
- Honey.
- Tea.
- Spices.
- Wine.

## First Product

FieldProof Studio.

Surfaces:

- Brand proof pages.
- Verifier capture studio.
- Standards and scorecard library.
- Producer profiles.
- Media asset library.

The consumer-facing proof page is the pull mechanism. The verifier app is the supply mechanism. The standards library is the credibility engine.

## Positioning

Do say:

- Low-cost field evidence for verified food origin.
- Turn farm visits into proof pages and marketing assets.
- Science-rooted capture for regenerative sourcing stories.
- A lightweight proof layer that can support certification later.

Do not lead with:

- Blockchain certification replacement.
- Full regenerative certification.
- Consumer app.
- Compliance software.

## What To Build First

Build the smallest profitable slice:

1. A brand creates a product batch proof page.
2. A verifier completes a guided field capture visit.
3. The capture workflow is mapped to one or more standards or scorecards.
4. The brand approves public media and story copy.
5. The product QR code points consumers to the proof page.
6. The brand can download approved assets for marketing.

## Pricing Hypothesis

Keep pricing below traditional certification and content-production budgets.

Possible pricing:

- $49-$199/month for small brand proof-page hosting.
- $150-$750 per origin capture visit, depending on geography and depth.
- 10-20% marketplace take rate on verifier work.
- $500-$2,000/month for co-ops, importers, or brands managing many products.

The first budget line is not "audit software." It is a blend of origin marketing, traceability, and lightweight verification.

## Strategic Constraint

FieldProof must avoid credibility theater.

The proof page should clearly distinguish:

- Producer story.
- Self-attested claim.
- Field-evidenced claim.
- Lab-supported claim.
- Third-party verified claim.
- Disputed or needs-revisit claim.

This keeps the product useful before formal certification while protecting trust.
